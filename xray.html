X-rays

X-rays were discovered by Wilhelm Roentgen in 1895. This groundbreaking discovery allowed scientists and doctors to see inside the human body without invasive procedures. X-rays are a form of electromagnetic radiation that can pass through objects, creating images based on different densities.

Today, X-rays are widely used in medical imaging, security screening, and industrial applications. They help diagnose fractures, detect tumors, and even analyze the structure of ancient artifacts. The invention of X-rays marked a major advancement in both medicine and science.
